Pantry Must Haves

There are some basic things that as a baker (or sometime baker) you should always have on hand.

Flour

If you go to a grocery store to the flour aisle, you will find many different types of flour. Which one do you choose? One important part of flour is how much protein it contains. Flour with a higher percentage of protein will create a harter flours, which are best for chewy crusty breads, flour with lower percentage of protein are best. As a beginner baker, your best bet will be to stick with All-Purpose flour.

Leaveners

These are what are used to cause baked items to rise. Different leaveners are - Yeast, Baking Soda, Baking Powder. . They are responsible for expanding the air bubbles that have been mixed, creamed, kneaded or whipped into the batter or dough. These millions of bubbles are trapped in the batter by the gluten structure formed and are enlarged by the leavener, either triggered by moisture and/or heat.

Sugar

There are many types of sugar used in baking. The most common is granulated sugar. There is also powdered sugar, often used for frosting. Brown sugar is sugar with molasses.

Salt

In baking, salt helps the fermentation of yeast, allowing it to raise. It also adds flavor.

Dairy

Eggs are important and are used in many different aspects of baking. They help to give baked goods texture and has protein to help with baking. It is important keep eggs refrigerated between 33-45 degrees fahrenheit Also, make sure eggs are fresh by checking the sell by date.

Butter, and Shortening

Both of these are used in baking. Butter is made from churning cream- it has 80% butterfat. Shortening however, is 100% fat.

Extracts and Flavorings

The most common extract used in baking is vanilla extract. This is used in many different recipes. There are also many different flavors you can buy to create flavors in your baked goods.

Spices/Add ins

Some basic spices to have are- cinnamon, cream of tarter, all-spice, chocolate
