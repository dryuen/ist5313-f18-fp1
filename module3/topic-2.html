These Simple Facts will Help You Get Started

What size pan? It is important to use the correct pan size as specified in a recipe. Different pan sizes will cook differently based on how spread out the item is. There is a big difference between an 8 inch round pan and a 9 inch round pan. Though it doesn’t seem much, the extra inch in diameter has a lot more volume, so the batter will spread thinner, and may not rise well. That being said, if you underfill a pan, the cake may crack. It’s best to use the correct size pan the recipe calls for.

What type pan do you use?

Metal

Metal pans are the best heat conductor, they are best for baking. There are many different types of metal pans.

Glass and Ceramic

These pans are not used in the commercial kitchen, though they are useful and great for home baking.

Springform

These are round pans that have 2 separate pieces, a flat base and a notched spring collar that you can close tightly then release and open to take out foods. These are useful for cheesecakes and other foods that are difficult to take out of pans.

Muffin pans

These are useful for small muffins or cupcakes. It is useful to use paper liners to be able to take all of the food out.

Tips- Most home bakers will bake with the third of fourth rung option from the bottom of the oven.
Keep an oven thermometer to check that oven is at proper temperature.

Reading the Recipe

As said before, it is important to read the recipe from beginning to end before starting. There may be some ingredients in the recipe not in the ingredients list (for example- water).

The ingredient list is usually written in the order of use.

When reading the ingredients, a comma is an important piece of information. When the comma comes after a measurement describing how the food is prepared, that means you measure then prepare that item.

For example- 4 ounces bittersweet chocolate, coarsely chopped means to first measure 4 ounces bittersweet chocolate, then chop it coarsely.

If there is not a comma, then that means that they are prepared then measured.

Is it finished?

Trust your instincts! When your food is close to being done, if it smells done, take a toothpick or butter knife and stick it down the middle. If your your toothpick or knife comes out clean, then it is done! You don’t want to overbake and burn!
