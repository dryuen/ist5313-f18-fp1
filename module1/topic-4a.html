Which Pan is Right for the Job?

Although it might seem convenient to cook all of your meals in the same pan, different types of pans have specific uses in the kitchen. Knowing when to use which pan can make a difference in your cooking and can save you money by ensuring the longevity of your cookware.

Here are four essential pans that you’ll want to have in your kitchen cabinet:

Saucepans

If you need to prepare a sauce for your meal, or make some kind of soup or gravy, your best bet is to use a saucepan. Usually round in appearance with a long handle and sometimes, a lid, saucepans are also ideal for boiling pasta or rice, among other things. It’s recommended that you have more than one saucepan of varying sizes to suit your cooking needs.

Stockpots

Stockpots, as the name suggests, are great for preparing stock. What’s really neat about stockpots is that they can also be used for other purposes, like boiling pasta and even making chili.

Skillets

Need to fry an omelet or stir-fry some vegetables? Then a skillet is just the pan you need. Commonly known as a frying pan, a skillet is a multi-purpose pan and a definite must have in every kitchen.

Saute

Saute pans usually have a long handle and a lid and are perfect for searing or browning meat.
