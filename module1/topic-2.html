Types of Measurement

Measurement is simple when you do it correctly. There are different types of measurement in cooking. We will start with the basics.

Liquid Measurement

When measuring liquids, you will use either a plastic or glass measuring cup. These are see-through so you can see exactly where the liquid will line up to. They have a handle and a spout to easily pour in liquids. The common measurements for liquid measuring cups are usually by cups, with increments showing fourths and thirds. You can get liquid measuring cups in the following sizes.

  • 8 cup
  • 4 cup
  • 2 cup
  • 1 cup

Dry Ingredients

Measuring cups for dry ingredients such as flour, rice, sugar, butter, oats, etc. You can find either plastic or metal cups and they will usually come in a set of sizes. When using a dry measuring cup, fill up the cup, then use a straight edge (the back of a knife works well) and take off the excess ingredients. The sizes you can find are as follows.

  • 1/4 cup
  • 1/3 cup
  • 1/2 cup
  • 2/3 cup
  • 3/4 cup
  • 1 cup

Measuring Spoons

When measuring small amounts of dry and liquid ingredient, it is useful to have two sets of measuring spoons. There are round measuring spoons and rectangular measuring spoons. Rectangular spoons are useful for getting into small spice jars. Measuring spoons usually come in the following sizes as a set, though there are other sizes available.

  • 1/4 teaspoon
  • 1/2 teaspoon
  • 1 teaspoon
  • 1 tablespoon

Scale

Kitchen scales are useful to have precise measurements of ingredients,especially dry ingredients such as flour. There can be a major disparity in how much is used depending on if it is packed in or if it is looser. Also, scales can do conversions!

Instant Read Thermometer

Having an instant-read thermometer is critical for making sure meat is cooked to safe temperatures. It is one of the most important tools to have when cooking your turkey on Thanksgiving! There are many out there, but you can find one at the grocery store for under $10. Make sure to wash the thermometer each time it is used to avoid passing bacteria from one meat to another.

Timer

Okay, this one is a given. Every recipe needs to be cooked for a certain amount of time. There are MANY timers out there, including some that are on ovens. If you are not near your kitchen though, it may be useful to set a timer on your phone...after all… it wouldn’t be fun for your smoke alarm to go off would it? Speaking of which…

Make sure your smoke alarm has batteries… you want to be prepared just in case!
